Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 27, 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how Guruji Tech Global LTD ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information when you visit our website https://www.gurujitechglobal.com, use our services, or communicate with us in any way.

By accessing or using our services, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with our practices, please do not use our services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

We may collect the following personal information from you:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, company name, and business address
  • Technical Information: IP address, browser type, device information, and website usage data
  • Communication Data: Messages, feedback, and correspondence you send to us
  • Business Information: Information about your IT infrastructure, systems, and requirements (when you become a client)
  • Payment Information: Billing details necessary for providing our services (processed securely through third-party payment processors)

1.2 Automatic Data Collection

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information including:

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Referring website and exit pages
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies (with your consent where required)

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain our IT services and support
  • To respond to your inquiries and requests
  • To process service requests and bookings (including Calendly consultations)
  • To improve our website, services, and customer experience
  • To send you service-related communications and updates
  • To comply with legal obligations and regulatory requirements
  • To protect our rights, property, and safety, as well as that of our clients and others
  • To analyze website usage and improve our marketing efforts (with your consent where required)

3. Legal Basis for Processing

Under applicable data protection laws (including UK GDPR), we process your personal information based on the following legal grounds:

  • Contract Performance: To fulfill our contractual obligations to you as a client
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Legitimate Interests: For our legitimate business interests in providing and improving our services
  • Consent: Where you have explicitly given consent for specific processing activities (such as marketing communications)

4. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your information with:

  • Service Providers: Third-party vendors who help us provide our services (e.g., cloud hosting providers, payment processors, email service providers)
  • Professional Advisors: Legal, accounting, or other professional advisors when necessary
  • Legal Authorities: When required by law, court order, or government regulation
  • Business Transfers: In connection with any merger, acquisition, or sale of company assets (with appropriate safeguards)

All third parties are required to implement appropriate security measures and process your data only as instructed by us.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Employee training on data protection and security
  • Incident response procedures for data breaches

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law. Generally:

  • Client Data: Retained for 6 years after the end of our business relationship (for legal and tax compliance)
  • Website Visitor Data: Retained for up to 26 months for analytics purposes
  • Inquiry Data: Retained for 2 years if no business relationship is established

7. Your Rights

Under data protection laws, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal information in certain circumstances
  • Right to Restriction: Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances
  • Right to Data Portability: Request transfer of your data to another organization
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided in Section 10.

8. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience and analyze website usage. We use:

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for website functionality (no consent required)
  • Analytics Cookies: To understand how visitors use our website (requires consent)
  • Marketing Cookies: To deliver personalized advertising (requires consent)

You can manage your cookie preferences through our cookie banner or by adjusting your browser settings.

9. International Data Transfers

Your personal information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the UK. When we transfer data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
  • Processing agreements with adequate data protection standards
  • Verification that third countries provide adequate data protection
